SSC EXAMS

SSC CGL

The SSC CGL (Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level) examination is a competitive exam conducted by the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) in India. It is one of the most sought-after government job exams in the country and is conducted to recruit candidates for various Group B and Group C posts in various government ministries, departments, and organizations.

Here are some details about the SSC CGL examination:

1. Eligibility Criteria:

~ Educational Qualification: A candidate must have a Bachelor's Degree from a recognized university or institution.

~ Age Limit: The age limit varies depending on the specific post you are applying for. Generally, the minimum age is 18 years, and the maximum age is 30 years. Age relaxations are provided for candidates belonging to reserved categories.

2. Tiers of Examination:

  • SSC CGL examination is conducted in four tiers:
    • Tier-I: Preliminary Examination (Objective Type)
    • Tier-II: Main Examination (Objective Type)
    • Tier-III: Descriptive Paper in English/Hindi (Pen and Paper mode)
    • Tier-IV: Skill Test/Computer Proficiency Test (as applicable for certain posts)

    3. Subjects Covered:

    • Tier-I: General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude, and English Comprehension.
    • Tier-II: Paper-I (Quantitative Abilities), Paper-II (English Language & Comprehension), Paper-III (Statistics), Paper-IV (General Studies-Finance & Economics).
    • Tier-III: Descriptive Paper in English/Hindi.
    • Tier-IV: Skill Test/Computer Proficiency Test.

    4. Exam Pattern:

    • Tier-I: 100 questions, 200 marks (2 hours)
    • Tier-II: Varies depending on the paper.
    • Tier-III: Descriptive paper, 100 marks (1 hour)
    • Tier-IV: Skill Test/Computer Proficiency Test (varies based on the specific post)

    5. Selection Process::

    • Candidates need to clear all four tiers of the examination.
    • Candidates are shortlisted for Tier-III based on their performance in Tier-I and Tier-II.
    • The final selection is based on the cumulative score of Tier-I, Tier-II, and Tier-III (if applicable).
    • Tier-IV: Skill Test/Computer Proficiency Test (varies based on the specific post)

    6. Application Process:

    • SSC CGL notification is released periodically, and candidates need to apply online through the SSC official website.

    7. Admit Card:

    • Admit cards for each tier are released separately, and candidates can download them from the official website.

    8.Result and Cut-off:

    • Results are declared for each tier separately, and cut-off marks are determined based on the number of vacancies and the performance of candidates.

    9. Pay Scale:

    • The pay scale varies depending on the post. SSC CGL offers lucrative salary packages for various government jobs.

    10.Job Posts:

    • SSC CGL exam qualifies candidates for a wide range of posts, including Assistant Audit Officer, Assistant Accounts Officer, Income Tax Inspector, Assistant Enforcement Officer, and many others.

    11.Preparation:

    • Candidates often prepare for SSC CGL with the help of coaching institutes, self-study, and practice through previous year's question papers.

    12.Important Dates:

    • The exam dates, application periods, and other important dates are mentioned in the official notification, which is released by SSC. ssc.nic.in
